Long-Context Aware Upcycling: A New Frontier for Hybrid LLM Scaling

April 28, 2026 6-minute read
Paper-Digest • 2026-04-28

arXiv: 2604.24715 · PDF

Authors: Parsa Ashrafi Fashi, Utkarsh Saxena, Mehdi Rezagholizadeh, Aref Jafari, Akash Haridas, Mingyu Yang, Vansh Bhatia, Guihong Li, Vikram Appia, Emad Barsoum

Affiliations: AMD

Primary category: cs.CL · all: cs.CL, cs.LG

Matched keywords: llm, reasoning, inference, serving, kv-cache, attention, transformer, post-train

TL;DR

HyLo upcycles pretrained Transformer LLMs into hybrid MLA+Mamba2/GatedDeltaNet architectures via staged long-context training and teacher distillation, extending context up to 32× and cutting KV-cache >90%, enabling 2M-token inference on vLLM while outperforming JetNemotron with 40× less training data.

Motivation

Hybrid sequence models pairing efficient Transformer blocks with linear-recurrent blocks promise cheaper long-context inference than pure Transformers, but nearly all public hybrids (Mamba-2, Jamba, JetNemotron, Zebra-Llama) are pretrained from scratch. That burns hundreds of billions of tokens and discards the enormous investment already sunk into Llama, Qwen, and similar Transformer checkpoints. Teams running vLLM or SGLang hit a concrete wall: Llama-3.2 baselines OOM beyond 64K context, so serving 256K+ documents or agent traces requires either expensive full pretraining of a new hybrid or aggressive KV eviction hacks that hurt quality. Prior upcycling attempts (Zebra-Llama, MOHAWK-style conversions) partially recover short-context accuracy but lag badly on genuine long-context benchmarks like RULER, and no recipe has convincingly pushed a converted checkpoint into the million-token regime. The authors argue the gap is not architectural but procedural: existing upcycling skips staged length curricula and teacher guidance, so optimization destabilizes when hybrid blocks replace full attention. HyLo targets that gap with a compact data budget (10B tokens) aimed squarely at practitioners who already own a strong 1B–3B Transformer and need long-context serving without a from-scratch restart.

Key Ideas

  • Upcycling recipe converting pretrained Transformers into hybrids of MLA + Mamba2 or Gated DeltaNet blocks.
  • MLA blocks initialized directly from pretrained attention weights rather than random init.
  • Staged long-context training with Yarn position interpolation to stretch context gradually.
  • Teacher-guided knowledge distillation to stabilize optimization during architectural swap.
  • 90%+ KV-cache reduction enabling 2M-token prefill/decode on vLLM where Llama OOMs past 64K.
  • Strong data efficiency: 10B tokens beats JetNemotron’s 400B-token from-scratch training.

Method

HyLo replaces a subset of a pretrained Transformer’s full-attention layers with two kinds of efficient blocks: Multi-Head Latent Attention (MLA) for the attention-preserving layers, and linear sequence blocks — either Mamba2 or Gated DeltaNet — for the rest. Crucially, MLA layers are initialized from the original attention weights (see figure below) instead of random, preserving short-context competence. Training proceeds in stages of increasing sequence length (up to 64K), with Yarn-based position interpolation bridging context extension. Throughout, a larger teacher model provides distillation signal to stabilize the swap from attention to linear blocks.

Figure 6 backs the MLA-initialization claim above: it shows how Q/K/V projections from a pretrained attention block are decomposed and mapped into MLA’s latent down-projection and head-specific up-projections. This weight reuse is what lets HyLo skip full pretraining — the MLA block inherits the teacher’s representational geometry rather than learning it from scratch, which is the lever that makes 10B-token upcycling viable.

Experiments

Evaluated at 1B and 3B scales on Llama- and Qwen-based backbones. Benchmarks: GSM8K and Lm-Harness commonsense for short context; RULER at 8K/16K/32K/64K and needle-in-haystack for long context. Baselines include Zebra-Llama, JetNemotron, and Llama-3.2-1B/3B. Inference measured on vLLM (TTFT, TPOT, max context). Ablations cover training sequence length, Yarn interpolation, and teacher size.

Results

HyLo extends usable context up to 32× and reaches 2M tokens on vLLM while Llama baselines OOM beyond 64K. HyLo-Qwen-1.7B (10B tokens) beats JetNemotron (400B tokens) on GSM8K, commonsense, and RULER-64K.

Figure 1 supports the “strong short- and long-context” headline: HyLo sits at or above Llama on short-context math while pulling clearly ahead of Zebra-Llama across all four RULER lengths (8K–64K), on a limited upcycling budget. The key takeaway is that the short-context regression typical of hybrid conversion is absent here.

Figure 2 substantiates the >90% KV-cache reduction claim. The 4MLA12M2 configuration matches Llama-3.2-1B on needle-in-haystack while using only 3.9% of the KV footprint, and surpasses Zebra-Llama; training at 64K further lifts performance beyond the base Llama on this synthetic retrieval task.

Figure 3 isolates the staged-training and Yarn choices from the Method. Applying Yarn improves long-context scores with only slight commonsense degradation, and training at longer sequence lengths preserves the extended context abilities — evidence that the staged curriculum, not just architecture, drives the long-context gains.

Figure 4 backs the teacher-guided distillation component: scaling teacher size yields a substantially larger boost on long-context tasks than on short-context commonsense, confirming distillation is load-bearing for the architectural swap rather than a cosmetic add-on.

Figure 5 quantifies the serving payoff at 3B scale on vLLM: TTFT and TPOT for the HyLo Llama-3.2-3B variant scale far more gracefully with context than the Transformer baseline, directly translating the KV savings into latency wins operators can measure.

Conclusion

For teams sitting on Llama/Qwen checkpoints who need long-context serving, HyLo is a credible alternative to from-scratch hybrid pretraining: 10B-token upcycling reaches 2M context on vLLM and beats a 400B-token hybrid (JetNemotron) on RULER-64K and GSM8K at 1.7B scale. The honest caveats are scale (only 1B/3B tested, no 7B+ numbers), a short-context commonsense dip under Yarn, and that all long-context evidence is on RULER and needle-in-haystack rather than real downstream tasks like repo-scale coding or multi-doc QA.

Novelty Check

  • Zebra-Llama — also upcycles Llama into a Mamba-hybrid; HyLo’s differentiators are MLA (not just linear swaps), Yarn staging, and teacher distillation, yielding materially better RULER.
  • JetNemotron — hybrid trained from scratch on 400B tokens; HyLo matches/beats it with 10B via upcycling, a genuinely different regime.
  • MOHAWK / Mamba-in-Llama distillation — shares the distillation-for-conversion idea; HyLo’s new piece is the MLA initialization path plus explicit long-context staging to 2M.

Genuinely new combination rather than a relabel, though each ingredient (MLA, Mamba2, Yarn, KD) is individually prior art.

Open Questions

  • Does the recipe scale to 7B/70B backbones, or do stability issues reappear?
  • How does HyLo fare on realistic long-context tasks (repo QA, agent traces) beyond RULER/NIAH?
  • Sensitivity to MLA/linear ratio (why 4MLA12M2?) and to teacher choice when no larger teacher exists.
  • Decode-time quality at 1M–2M contexts is claimed feasible but accuracy there is unreported.

Original abstract

Hybrid sequence models that combine efficient Transformer components with linear sequence modeling blocks are a promising alternative to pure Transformers, but most are still pretrained from scratch and therefore fail to reuse existing Transformer checkpoints. We study upcycling as a practical path to convert pretrained Transformer LLMs into hybrid architectures while preserving short-context quality and improving long-context capability. We call our solution \emph{HyLo} (HYbrid LOng-context): a long-context upcycling recipe that combines architectural adaptation with efficient Transformer blocks, Multi-Head Latent Attention (MLA), and linear blocks (Mamba2 or Gated DeltaNet), together with staged long-context training and teacher-guided distillation for stable optimization. HyLo extends usable context length by up to $32\times$ through efficient post-training and reduces KV-cache memory by more than $90%$, enabling up to 2M-token prefill and decoding in our \texttt{vLLM} inference stack, while comparable Llama baselines run out of memory beyond 64K context. Across 1B- and 3B-scale settings (Llama- and Qwen-based variants), HyLo delivers consistently strong short- and long-context performance and significantly outperforms state-of-the-art upcycled hybrid baselines on long-context evaluations such as RULER. Notably, at similar scale, HyLo-Qwen-1.7B trained on only 10B tokens significantly outperforms JetNemotron (trained on 400B tokens) on GSM8K, Lm-Harness common sense reasoning and RULER-64K.
